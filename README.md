Low Power Conditional Capture Flip-Flop (CCFF)

This project presents a low-power flip-flop design using 16nm CMOS technology.
The Conditional Capture Flip-Flop (CCFF) reduces unnecessary switching activity when input data remains unchanged.
This leads to significant dynamic power savings compared to conventional flip-flops.
The design operates at 0.9V and 12.5MHz with an average power consumption of 4.08µW.
It achieves a fast clock-to-Q delay of 85.7ps and a Power-Delay Product of 0.35fJ.
Simulation results verify correct functionality and improved energy efficiency.
The CCFF shows up to 46% power reduction at moderate switching activity.
Post-layout analysis confirms stability and performance under realistic conditions.
The design is scalable and suitable for low-power VLSI systems and register banks.
